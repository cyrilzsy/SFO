Staple Food Ordinance Model: Help

Bottom bar
Start you tour here
  • Click on Staple Food Selection button in the bottom bar
  • Choose the food you want to simulate by click the blue buttons on the top or the left of each food (Muilt-selection allowed)
  • Choose the food you want to change the characteristics in the radio button on the right of the food (one food each time)
  • Click the Staple Food Selection button again or click on the x button on the top right of the bar to retract the selection window
  • Click on Plot Selection button to show the availible plots for the result
  • Choose the results that you are interested in by click the blue buttons on the left of each variable
  • Click the Plot Selection button again or click on the x button on the top right of the bar to retract the selection window
Upper menu
Enter Your Conditions
  • Use the Sliders in the Upper menu to change the characteristic of each food
  • You will see changes in the Result area directly
Graphs

Following descriptions of plots are in the same order of the Plot Selection, from top to bottom, if you selected all the plots in that menu, you will see from Plot 1 to Plot 10 located from left to right, from top to bottom

Plot 1
  • Y_supply
  • Unit: units/week
  • Amount supplied to the store each week
Plot 2
  • S_actual
  • Unit: units
  • Actual stock in store at the beginning of each week
Plot 3
  • Y_demand
  • Unit: units/week
  • Customer demand, how many of this food does the customer want each week
Plot 4
  • Y_cust
  • Unit: units/week
  • Amount of food actually purchased by customer each week
Plot 5
  • Y_waste
  • Unit: units/week
  • Amount wasted from store each week
Plot 6
  • M_profit
  • Unit: $/week
  • Weekly profit, how much money earned by the store (Costs deducted)
Plot 7
  • M_storage
  • Unit: $/week
  • Storage cost per week, based on how many foods are actually stored in the store
Plot 8
  • M_supply
  • Unit: $/week
  • Weekly purchase cost for the store (to supplier), how much does the store spend to buy the food each week
Plot 9
  • M_delivery
  • Unit: $/week
  • Weekly cost of delivery, how much does the store spend on delivery related actions
Plot 10
  • M_cust
  • Unit: $/week
  • Weekly revenue from customer (to store), how much money does the store earned from customer (cost not deducted)
Plot 11
  • S_actual_end
  • Unit: units
  • Actual stock in store at the end of each week
